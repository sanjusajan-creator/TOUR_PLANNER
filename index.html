Agra Fort

The Agra Fort (Qila Agra) is a historical Mughal fort in the city of Agra, also known as Agra's Red Fort. Mughal emperor Humayun was crowned here in 1530. It was later renovated by Akbar from 1565, with the present structure completed in 1573. It served as the main residence of the Mughal dynasty until 1638, when the capital shifted to Delhi. In 1983, it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is located 2.5 km northwest of the Taj Mahal and is often described as a walled city.

Travel Mode: 5-10 min via E-Rickshaw/Uber/Auto / 15 min walk

The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal ('Crown of the Palaces') is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra. It was commissioned in 1631 by Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. The monument was built by around 20,000 artisans and completed in 1653. It is considered a masterpiece of symmetrical Mughal architecture. In 1983, it was recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Today, it stands as a global symbol of eternal love.
